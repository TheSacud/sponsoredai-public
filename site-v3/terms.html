4. Description of the Service

SAI wraps terminal workflows such as Codex CLI, Claude Code, and long-running commands. When the agent is quiet long enough, SAI can show one sponsor line and record the small set of event fields needed to bill and settle it. An optional desktop overlay (sai overlay) can float one sponsor banner over a supported desktop app, such as Claude Desktop or the Codex app, using local window state to show it only while you are actively attending that app; it is dismissible and controlled from the tray menu and sai config.

For Developers, the backend ledger is the source of truth. A local wallet display is just a cache of what the backend believes can be pending, available, settled, spent, or revoked.

For Sponsors, SAI provides campaign creation, review, bids, Stripe Checkout, funded balance, delivery rules, and aggregate reporting for qualified placements and eligible clicks.

There may be days with no funded demand. There may be no available provider spend. Supported AI tools may change. SAI does not guarantee earnings, delivery, compatibility, uptime, or return on ad spend.

6. Developer Credits

6.1 How Credits Accrue

Credits only come from real sponsor money. A campaign must be live, approved, funded, selected by the backend, rendered locally, visible long enough, and accepted by the current metric contract. Example cards, seeded dev data, duplicate events, expired placements, invalid clicks, and fraud-filtered events pay nothing.

6.2 Cash-Out Eligibility

Eligible available or settled balance can be requested as cash-out through Stripe Connect once the payout account, threshold, settlement holds, and review requirements are satisfied. Cash-out is not instant or guaranteed; SAI can hold, reject, fail, reverse, or claw back payouts where payment, fraud, abuse, refund, dispute, sanctions, tax, or legal risk applies.

6.3 Settlement, Caps, and Corrections

Credits can sit in pending state before becoming available. Settlement holds, hourly caps, campaign caps, account caps, fraud reviews, corrections, and clawbacks are part of the product. Refunds, disputes, failed payments, duplicate events, invalid clicks, sponsor non-payment, or abuse can freeze, reduce, revoke, or reverse credits or payout requests.

6.4 Spend Through the Gateway

When wallet spend is enabled, the gateway can provision a provider key with a limit tied to your spendable balance. Provider outages, usage counters, pricing, rate limits, model availability, and policy decisions can all affect whether a credit can be spent at a given moment.
