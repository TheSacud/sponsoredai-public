Privacy

Privacy Policy

SAI's privacy rule is simple: measure the pause, not the work. Whether the sponsor line shows in a terminal or as a small banner from the optional SAI desktop overlay over a supported app, SAI validates the wait-state event without sending us prompts, code, terminal logs, model responses, repository URLs, full file paths, shell history, or the titles or contents of any window.

Common questions

Fair questions

So… this is adware?

It’s one line of text, in a moment you were staring at a spinner anyway, that pays you in AI credits. It appears in interactive terminals, or as a small banner the optional SAI desktop overlay floats over a supported app such as Claude Desktop or the Codex app — never in CI. It is dismissable, capped in frequency, and you control it from the tray menu, with sai config set frequency low, or sai config kill-switch on, which stops every sponsor surface instantly.

Can you read my code or prompts?

No — and not as a policy promise, as a mechanism. The runner watches output timing through a PTY, never content. Events are sanitized against a fixed schema where code_uploaded, prompt_uploaded and logs_uploaded are hard-coded false. Run sai privacy schema to read the whole contract.

What is a credit actually worth?

One credit pays for one US dollar of AI model usage, prepaid by a sponsor. Credits are server-side developer earnings backed by paid sponsor placements; the local wallet is only a display cache. You spend them on model calls — OpenRouter for now — straight from your terminal. During Beta, credited earnings are capped at USD 5 per installation per hour to limit abuse.

1. Scope

This page covers the SAI CLI, sponsoredai.dev, the sponsor console, backend APIs, the credit ledger, the gateway, and related tools.

Read it with the Terms of Service. If a checkout, order form, or signed agreement has more specific privacy language for a transaction, that more specific language controls that transaction.

2. Local Processing and the Privacy Boundary

SAI runs on your machine. It looks at timing and local display state: is the terminal interactive, is the agent waiting, did the sponsor line render, and did it stay visible long enough? It does not send SAI the content of the work happening in that terminal.

The optional desktop overlay (sai overlay) floats one sponsor banner over a supported desktop app, such as Claude Desktop or the Codex app. To show it honestly and bill only a genuinely visible, attended view, it reads local window state through the operating system: whether a supported app is in the foreground, whether its own banner is actually on screen, and how long since your last keyboard or mouse input. These signals are evaluated locally to decide when to show the banner and when an impression qualifies. SAI does not read the titles or contents of any window, and does not upload which app you were using beyond the surface label below.

Any sponsor logo shown in the banner is fetched and cached by SAI server-side and served from sponsoredai.dev, so the sponsor's own servers never receive your request, IP address, user agent, or the timing of when the ad appeared.

The event contract is intentionally small. If a sponsor event includes fields outside the allowed schema, SAI rejects or strips them before recording. You can inspect the contract locally with sai privacy schema.

// sponsor event payload shape (terminal surface)
{ "surface": "cli_agent_wait", "tool": "codex", "event": "agent_thinking", "duration_bucket": "10-30s", "terminal_interactive": true, "ci": false, "country": "PT", "code_uploaded": false, "prompt_uploaded": false, "logs_uploaded": false }

Transport identifiers can also travel with events: installation hash, CLI version, session ID, placement ID, campaign ID, signature, and click token. They let the backend prevent replay, settle credits, and attribute clicks. They are not prompts, commands, paths, code, terminal output, or shell history.

The desktop overlay uses the same payload with two differences: "surface" is "desktop_overlay", and the attested field is "attended_interactive" instead of "terminal_interactive". The foreground-app identity, on-screen visibility, and idle time it reads stay on your machine; only this small allowlisted schema reaches the backend.

3. What Reaches the Backend

3.1 Account and Sponsor Data

If you sign in or create a sponsor account, SAI stores the basics needed to run that account: email, name, profile picture if your login provider returns one, authentication provider ID, session data, role, sponsor ID, campaign details, destination URLs, bids, budgets, checkout status, and support messages.

3.2 Developer and Installation Data

When the CLI or desktop overlay talks to the backend, SAI can process installation hashes, session identifiers, CLI version, selected tool, the surface (interactive terminal or desktop overlay) and whether the view was attended, placement IDs, event type, duration bucket, approximate country, click tokens, credit balances, settlement state, gateway provisioning state, and provider usage counters needed to debit spendable credits.

3.3 Payment Data

Stripe handles sponsor payments. SAI keeps payment references: Stripe checkout session IDs, payment status, amounts, campaign IDs, top-up IDs, refund or dispute status, and accounting ledger entries. SAI does not store full card numbers.

3.4 Gateway Data

When wallet spend is enabled, the backend can provision or resize a model-provider key and read provider usage counters so the ledger can debit credits. Your model call goes from your machine to the provider with the runtime key. SAI's backend does not proxy or read the prompt and response in that spend flow.

4. Website Analytics, Logs, and Cookies

The site and backend keep ordinary server logs: time, path, status code, user agent, referrer, and IP-derived network information. Those logs support security, abuse prevention, debugging, uptime work, and aggregate web analytics.

Sponsor and admin pages use cookies or similar session tokens so sign-in and OAuth flows work. Public marketing and legal pages can be read without signing in.

Current web analytics are server-side log analytics. SAI does not sell visitor data. If third-party analytics or advertising pixels are added later, this policy will be updated and any required controls will be added.

5. How We Use Information

  • Keep the CLI, backend, sponsor console, market, gateway, and website running.
  • Issue placements, verify five-second qualified views, record eligible clicks, and update the backend credit ledger.
  • Handle sponsor checkout, wallet top-ups, refunds, disputes, campaign state, and accounting entries.
  • Provision spend-limited provider keys and debit credits from provider usage counters.
  • Find fraud, duplicate events, cap evasion, payment risk, replay attempts, and security incidents.
  • Send service, account, security, billing, and support messages.
  • Improve reliability, pricing, placement behavior, and product fit.
  • Meet legal, tax, sanctions, accounting, and dispute obligations.

6. Google User Data

If you choose Google sign-in, SAI receives basic OpenID profile data: verified email address, name, profile picture, and Google subject ID. That data is used for account login, sponsor/admin access, and account security.

  • Google user data is not used for ad targeting, retargeting, or profiling.
  • Google user data is not sold or shared with data brokers.
  • Google user data is not used to train AI or machine learning models.
  • Google user data is used only for authentication and related account functionality.

7. How We Share Information

SAI does not sell personal information. It does rely on service providers and counterparties:

  • Stripe handles checkout, payment processing, refunds, disputes, and payment compliance.
  • Google handles OAuth sign-in when you choose that login path.
  • Model providers such as OpenRouter may be used for provider key provisioning and usage synchronization when wallet spend is enabled.
  • Hosting, infrastructure, security, logging, and email providers support day-to-day operation.
  • Sponsors receive aggregate campaign reporting: served, rendered, qualified, clicked, and spent counts. They do not receive prompts, source code, terminal output, or full developer work context.
  • Authorities, courts, counterparties, and advisors may receive information when needed for legal compliance, safety, fraud prevention, corporate transactions, or dispute resolution.

8. Data Retention and Deletion

Account, campaign, payment, credit, and accounting records stay as long as SAI needs them to operate the Service and handle business, legal, tax, audit, fraud-prevention, and dispute needs. Financial and abuse-prevention records can be kept for up to seven years, or longer if law requires it.

Server logs and aggregate analytics are kept for operations and security. Aggregated or de-identified data can be kept without a fixed deadline when it no longer reasonably identifies a person.

You can ask for deletion. Some records may remain if deleting them would break fraud prevention, security, accounting, legal compliance, or dispute handling.

9. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on where you live, you may be able to request access, correction, deletion, portability, restriction, or objection to some processing. California and other U.S. state laws can add rights to know, correct, delete, and opt out of sale or sharing.

SAI does not sell personal information. Send rights requests to support@sponsoredai.dev. We may verify identity or authority before responding.

10. Security

SAI uses practical safeguards: encrypted connections, signed sessions, access controls, rate limits, Stripe webhook signature checks, narrow telemetry schemas, and separation between local work content and backend events.

No service is perfectly secure. Protect your machine, npm environment, terminal sessions, API keys, browser sessions, and SAI config.

11. Children

SAI is not for children under 18. If you believe a child sent information to SAI, contact us and we will review it.

12. Changes to This Policy

This policy will change as SAI changes. The "Last updated" date shows the current version. For material changes, SAI will try to notify users through the website, account interface, CLI, or email.
